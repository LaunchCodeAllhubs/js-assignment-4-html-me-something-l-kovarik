If on a Browser's Page a Reader

By now you have most likely witnessed the power of AI to generate 10-page term papers explaining the reasons why (or why not) a hot dog should be considered a sandwich. It can generate research reports, pages of code, or instructions on the best way to spatchcock a chicken. Is there anything AI can't do? How about spin a good yarn?

Italo Calvino's If on a Winter's Night a Traveler introduces the first chapters of ten different novels within a frame of an overarching story- leaving the reader wondering what the heck happened next. Do you think AI will be just as good at generating a hook? Let's find out.

All stories generated by feeding prompts to ChatGPT, all images generated using Adobe Firefly, all code generated by me.

It was a bright and sunny day....

The Western

The town of Deadwood Springs had seen its fair share of lawlessness, but on that scorching summer day, it seemed the devil himself had come to claim the dusty streets. A band of notorious outlaws, led by the cunning and ruthless outlaw known as "Scarface" Malone, rode into town, leaving a trail of chaos in their wake. The townsfolk cowered behind locked doors, fearing for their lives as the gang wreaked havoc.

Amid the chaos, a lone figure emerged from the shadows. Lucas "The Lone Bullet" Jackson, a gunslinger of legendary repute, had ridden into Deadwood Springs seeking nothing but a quiet drink. But fate had other plans for him. As he stepped into the deserted saloon, the air thick with tension, he knew trouble had found him once again.

Scarface Malone's cold eyes locked onto Lucas, a sinister grin spreading across his scarred face. "Well, well, well, if it ain't the famous Lone Bullet," he sneered. "I've heard a lot about you, gunslinger. Care to join my little party?" The barrel of Scarface's revolver glinted in the dim light, and Lucas knew he had walked straight into a deadly ambush.

The Sci-Fi Novel

In the distant reaches of the cosmos, where stars swirled like celestial dancers, a thrilling saga was about to unfold. The year was 2357, and humanity had ventured far beyond the confines of Earth, exploring the uncharted corners of the universe. The starship "Aurora Nova," a marvel of futuristic engineering, soared through the cosmos, carrying a crew of daring explorers led by Captain Alex Mercer.

Their mission was to chart new planets and seek out signs of intelligent life among the myriad galaxies. But as the "Aurora Nova" approached a particularly enigmatic nebula, strange readings flickered on their scanners. Unbeknownst to them, a mysterious energy signature emanated from within the nebula, beckoning them closer.

As they navigated the shimmering tendrils of the nebula, the ship's systems began to act erratically, sending alarms blaring through the corridors. The crew exchanged nervous glances, unsure of what lay ahead. Suddenly, the "Aurora Nova" came to a jarring halt, as if caught in an invisible web. The ship's viewports revealed an astonishing sight - a gargantuan spacecraft, unlike anything they had ever encountered, loomed before them, its sleek, metallic surface reflecting the dazzling lights of the nebula. Before Captain Mercer could issue a command, a voice, neither human nor alien, resonated through the ship's intercom, uttering a cryptic message that sent shivers down their spines. "You have entered forbidden territory. Turn back now, or face the consequences."

The Murder Mystery

In the heart of a fog-shrouded city, where shadows danced along dimly-lit alleys, a chilling mystery was about to unfold. Detective Benjamin Drake, a seasoned investigator with a reputation for solving the most baffling cases, found himself facing a puzzle like no other. The mansion atop Hawthorne Hill was adorned with secrets, and the untimely death of its reclusive owner, Sir Reginald Hawthorne, held the key to unraveling the enigma.

As Detective Drake stepped through the grand entrance of the mansion, he was met with a scene of eerie silence. The air hung heavy with tension, and the scent of an old crime lingered in every corner. Sir Reginald's lifeless body lay sprawled on the ornate Persian rug, a single bullet wound in his chest, and the glint of a silver pocket watch clutched in his hand.

A muffled whisper echoed through the hallways, making the detective's instincts tingle. He followed the sound, his steps cautious yet resolute, leading him to a hidden passage obscured behind a large portrait. The passage led him to a secret chamber, adorned with obscure symbols and ancient relics. And there, standing amidst the mystique, was a shadowy figure in the corner, their face concealed by darkness. As Detective Drake called out, demanding they reveal themselves, the figure turned, and a flash of moonlight revealed something shocking - it was a face he knew all too well. But how could this be? The person standing before him was supposed to have died years ago.

The Verdict

Have you had enough, or are you thirsty for more? Can ChatGPT hold a candle to your favorite author? Only one way to find out what happened- throw on your duster, wander on down this dark alley, and see what you find.
